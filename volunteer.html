Lizzie Biddle

Lizzie Biddle holds a Master of Arts in Peace and Conflict Studies from the UNC Greensboro. She worked with immigrants and refugees in Greensboro at the University of North Carolina Greensboro’s Center for New North Carolinians for ten years. Throughout her time at the CNNC, She spent the bulk of her time overseeing services and programs in four different community-based sites in area neighborhoods with large numbers of newcomers. As part of her studies, she completed a graduate student internship with the U.S. State Department Bureau of Population, Refugees and Migration in Washington DC, Lizzie has traveled extensively internationally with the specific goal of studying forced migration, Lizzie now works as a trainer and organizer with the Racial Equity Institute.

Diannie H.B. Welsh

Board Member

is the Hayes Distinguished Professor of Entrepreneurship and Founding Director of the Entrepreneurship Programs at The University of North Carolina Greensboro. Dianne has held three endowed chairs and started three entrepreneurship programs/centers. She is the 2015 Fulbright-Hall Distinguished Chair for Entrepreneurship for Central Europe. She has visited or lectured in 50 plus countries, including the University of Venda, South Africa where she trained 30 professors in entrepreneurship. Dr. Welsh won the Legacy Award from the Global Consortium of Entrepreneurship Centers and the Barbara Hollander Award for Lifetime Contributions to Family Business from the Family Firm Institute.

Siddiga Ahmed

President

Siddiga is a Social Worker, and community leader. She has bachelor’s in social work from UNCG and a master’s in clinical social work from the Joint program of UNCG&A&T. She has worked with refugees and immigrants since 2007 assisting many families in the Triad area. She had been Elected to be the chair of the International Advisory Committee of Greensboro in 2019. She is the founder of Giving Hands US which is an attempt to serve those in need on larger scale. She had won Minerva Award for Distinguished Service from UNCG and outstanding volunteer Award from GIA. She has served on several nonprofit boards and committees including Greensboro African public Action Committee and Habitat for Humanity.
